# Nets and Prisms

## Learning Goal
- I can explain how a net folds into a prism.
- I can match each face in a net to the correct face on the solid.
- I can use the net to prepare a surface-area calculation.

## Introduction
A prism is a three-dimensional solid, but a net is a flat drawing.

The power of a net is that it shows every face at once.

That helps students reason about:

- which faces are congruent
- which edges meet when the solid folds
- how to organize a surface-area calculation

## Key Vocabulary
**Net**: A flat arrangement of all the faces of a solid figure.

**Prism**: A solid with two matching, parallel bases.

**Base**: One of the matching faces of a prism.

**Lateral faces**: The side faces that connect the bases.

**Congruent**: Having the same shape and size.

## How a Net Connects to a Prism
When a net folds:

- each rectangle becomes one face of the solid
- matching edges line up
- no faces overlap

If the rectangles do not line up correctly, the net will not fold into the intended prism.

## Worked Example 1: Identifying Matching Faces
A rectangular prism has dimensions:

- length `6`
- width `4`
- height `2`

Its net must include:

- two `6 x 4` rectangles for the top and bottom
- two `6 x 2` rectangles for the front and back
- two `4 x 2` rectangles for the left and right sides

Looking for those matching pairs helps confirm that the net is complete.

## Worked Example 2: Using a Net to Find Surface Area
Once the net is laid out, find the area of each rectangle:

- `6 x 4 = 24`
- `6 x 2 = 12`
- `4 x 2 = 8`

Each rectangle appears twice, so add:

`2(24) + 2(12) + 2(8)`

`48 + 24 + 16 = 88`

So the prism has surface area `88 square units`.

## Common Mistake
Students sometimes count only the faces they can see on the folded solid.

A net prevents that mistake because it shows all six faces at the same time.

## Summary
Nets turn a three-dimensional prism into an organized two-dimensional plan.

To use nets well:

1. identify the bases and side faces
2. check which faces are congruent
3. use the net to keep track of every face
4. add areas only after the face list is complete
