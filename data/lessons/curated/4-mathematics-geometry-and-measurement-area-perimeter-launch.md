# Area/Perimeter

## Learning Goal
- I can explain the difference between area and perimeter.
- I can calculate perimeter by adding side lengths.
- I can calculate area of a rectangle by multiplying length and width.

## Introduction
Area and perimeter are both measurements, but they answer different questions.

- perimeter asks how far it is around a shape
- area asks how much space is inside a shape

Students often confuse them because both use the same rectangle. The key is to decide whether the problem is about the border or the inside.

## Key Vocabulary
**Perimeter**: The total distance around a shape.

**Area**: The amount of space inside a shape.

**Rectangle**: A four-sided shape with four right angles.

## Worked Example 1: Perimeter
A rectangle has length `6` units and width `4` units.

Add all four sides:

`6 + 4 + 6 + 4 = 20`

So the perimeter is `20` units.

## Worked Example 2: Area
Use the same rectangle.

Multiply length times width:

`6 x 4 = 24`

So the area is `24 square units`.

## Common Mistake
If a student multiplies `6 x 4` when the question asks for distance around the edge, they found area instead of perimeter.

A good habit is to ask:

- around the shape?
- or inside the shape?

## Summary
Area and perimeter use the same figures but answer different questions.

1. Perimeter measures the distance around.
2. Area measures the space inside.
3. Rectangles are useful because students can calculate both and compare how they change.
