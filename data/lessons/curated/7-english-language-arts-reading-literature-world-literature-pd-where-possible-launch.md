# World Literature (PD where possible)

## Learning Goal
- I can identify how setting and cultural details shape a literary excerpt.
- I can explain how a character's perspective changes across the text.
- I can support a theme using precise textual evidence.

## Introduction
World literature often gives readers a close look at a specific place, tradition, or community. Strong readers pay attention to those details, but they also ask what larger idea the story is developing through them.

In this lesson, you will read a short excerpt about a student helping with a night market tradition. As you read, focus on three questions:

1. Which details are important in the setting?
2. How does the main character feel at first, and how does that change?
3. What bigger idea does the story develop?

## Key Vocabulary
**Setting**: The time and place of a story.

**Perspective**: A character's way of seeing and understanding events.

**Tradition**: A practice passed down over time.

**Motif**: A repeated image or detail that helps build meaning.

**Theme**: An important idea or message in a text.

## Text Focus: Literary Excerpt
Read the excerpt below. The paragraph numbers will help you locate the strongest evidence.

### Excerpt from "Lantern Street"
**Paragraph 1.** By the time Sora reached Lantern Street, her aunt had already hung the first row of painted lamps above the food stalls. Steam from soup pots drifted into the cool evening air, and the stone road glowed orange and gold where the paper light touched it. Sora set her school binder under the folding table and told herself she would help for one hour, no more.

**Paragraph 2.** Every year before the market opened, the oldest shopkeepers lit the lamps in silence. Sora used to love that part when she was younger. Now she mostly noticed how long it took. Her classmates were at home finishing science projects, and she still had graphs to label before morning.

**Paragraph 3.** "Take these to the tea stand," her aunt said, handing her a tray of small ceramic cups. Sora obeyed, weaving through neighbors who greeted her by name. At the tea stand, Mr. Ito nodded toward the lamps above them. "Your grandmother painted the blue ones during the flood year," he said. "People remember that light."

**Paragraph 4.** Sora looked up. The blue lamps were uneven, one darker than the next, and one had a crease across the painted river. For the first time that evening, the market did not feel slow to her. It felt layered, as if many different years were hanging above the street at once.

**Paragraph 5.** When her aunt asked whether she needed to leave, Sora reached under the table, touched the edge of her binder, and pushed it farther back. "I can stay through opening," she said. Then, after the lamps were fully lit, she added, "Maybe through closing."

## Tracking the Character's Perspective
At first, Sora sees the market as an interruption.

Evidence:

- she plans to help for "one hour, no more"
- she thinks about the graphs waiting in her binder
- she notices how long the ceremony takes

These details show distance. She is physically present, but mentally focused elsewhere.

By the end of the excerpt, her perspective changes.

Evidence:

- the market feels "layered"
- she decides to stay longer
- she pushes the binder farther back

That action with the binder matters because it shows the change without explaining it directly.

## Setting Details and Theme
The setting details help build the story's meaning:

- painted lamps
- steam from the soup pots
- neighbors greeting Sora by name
- the blue lamps painted during the flood year

These details show that the market is not only a place to buy and sell goods. It is also a place of memory and connection.

A strong theme for this excerpt is:

"Traditions can help people feel part of a larger story."

That theme fits because Sora begins by thinking only about her own schedule, but the market helps her see herself as part of something older and shared.

## Model Analysis: Best Evidence for Theme
Suppose the question is:

"Which detail best supports the idea that Sora begins to value the tradition more deeply?"

A strong choice is the line in Paragraph 4:

"It felt layered, as if many different years were hanging above the street at once."

Why is this strong evidence?

- It shows a change in Sora's thinking.
- It connects the market to memory and history.
- It helps explain why she later chooses to stay.

A weaker choice would be the description of steam and glowing road in Paragraph 1. That detail builds the setting well, but it does not show Sora's change in perspective as directly.

## Common Mistake: Noticing Setting Without Interpreting It
A weak response might say:

"The story takes place at a lantern market."

That is correct, but it only identifies the setting.

A stronger response explains what the setting does:

"The lantern market helps Sora understand that the tradition connects many years of family and community memory. This is why her perspective changes from impatience to appreciation."

## Summary
When you read world literature:

1. notice the important setting and cultural details
2. track how the character responds to them
3. identify the moment when the perspective changes
4. connect those details to a larger theme

In this lesson, the lantern market mattered because it changed the way Sora saw her evening. It stopped feeling like a delay and started feeling like part of a larger shared story.
