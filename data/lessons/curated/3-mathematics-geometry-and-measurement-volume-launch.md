# Volume

## Learning Goal
- I can explain that volume measures space inside a solid.
- I can count unit cubes to find volume.
- I can describe a prism by its layers of cubes.

## Introduction
Volume tells how much space is inside a solid figure.

One good way to understand volume is to build a shape from unit cubes and count them.

## Visual Model
![Rectangular prism built from unit cubes](/images/lessons/math/volume_prism.svg)

Students can see that a prism is built from equal cubes arranged in rows, columns, and layers.

## Key Vocabulary
**Volume**: The space inside a solid figure.

**Unit cube**: A cube that represents one cubic unit.

**Layer**: One flat group of cubes in a solid.

## Worked Example 1: Count the Cubes
A small prism has:

- `3` cubes on the bottom row
- `2` rows in each layer
- `2` layers

First find one layer:

`3 x 2 = 6` cubes

Then count both layers:

`6 + 6 = 12` cubes

The volume is `12 cubic units`.

## Worked Example 2: Compare Two Solids
Prism A has `8` unit cubes.

Prism B has `12` unit cubes.

Prism B has the greater volume because it takes up more inside space.

## Common Mistake
Students sometimes count only the cubes they can see from the front.

Remember to count:

- front cubes
- back cubes
- cubes in every layer

## Summary
Volume is the space inside a solid, and unit cubes help make that idea visible.

To find volume well:

1. look for rows, columns, and layers
2. count every cube
3. label the answer in cubic units
