# What is Surface Area?

## Learning Goal
- I can explain surface area as the total area of the outside faces of a solid.
- I can use a net to organize the faces of a prism.
- I can label my answer in square units instead of cubic units.

## Introduction
Surface area is about covering the outside of a solid figure.

If you imagine wrapping a present or covering a box with paper, you are thinking about surface area, not volume.

In this lesson, focus on three questions:

1. What counts as part of the outside of a prism?
2. How does a net help me see every face?
3. Why is surface area measured in square units?

## Key Vocabulary
**Surface area**: The total area of all the outside faces of a solid figure.

**Net**: A two-dimensional layout that shows all the faces of a solid.

**Face**: A flat surface of a three-dimensional figure.

**Prism**: A solid with two matching bases and flat side faces.

**Square unit**: A unit used to measure area, such as `cm^2` or `in^2`.

## Why Nets Help
When a prism is folded, some faces are hard to see at the same time.

A net unfolds the solid so every face is visible.

That makes it easier to:

- find the area of one face
- identify matching faces
- add all of the outside areas without missing one

## Worked Example 1: Surface Area of a Small Prism
A rectangular prism has:

- length `4`
- width `2`
- height `3`

Find the area of each pair of matching faces.

Top and bottom:

`4 x 2 = 8`

There are `2` of these faces:

`2 x 8 = 16`

Front and back:

`4 x 3 = 12`

There are `2` of these faces:

`2 x 12 = 24`

Left and right:

`2 x 3 = 6`

There are `2` of these faces:

`2 x 6 = 12`

Add the areas:

`16 + 24 + 12 = 52`

So the surface area is `52 square units`.

## Worked Example 2: Thinking About Wrapping Paper
A gift box is `5` inches long, `3` inches wide, and `2` inches tall.

If the problem asks how much paper is needed to cover the outside, surface area is the correct measure.

That is because paper covers faces, and faces are two-dimensional.

## Common Mistake
Students sometimes use cubic units because the figure is three-dimensional.

That is incorrect for surface area.

Use this check:

- outside covering -> square units
- inside space -> cubic units

## Summary
Surface area measures the outside of a solid, not the inside.

To solve well:

1. use a net or list the faces
2. find the area of each face
3. add the areas carefully
4. label the answer in square units
