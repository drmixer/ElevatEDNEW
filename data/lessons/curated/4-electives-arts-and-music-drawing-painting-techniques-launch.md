# Drawing/Painting Techniques Launch Lesson

## Learning Goal
- I can use color, line, and texture to make artwork more interesting.
- I can explain how artists create the feeling of depth.
- I can choose one technique and use it on purpose in my own work.

## Introduction
Artists make flat paper feel full of space, texture, and movement by making intentional choices. In this launch lesson, students look closely at how color, mark-making, and placement can change the way an artwork feels.

## Key Ideas
- Warm colors often feel closer and more energetic.
- Cool colors can feel calmer or farther away.
- Texture can be created with repeated marks, layered paint, or varied tools.
- Overlap and size can help create depth.

## Worked Example
For example, if an artist draws a large tree in the front of a picture and a smaller mountain near the top of the page, the mountain looks farther away even though both are drawn on the same flat surface.

If the artist also uses rough, short pencil marks on the tree bark and soft blue paint on the mountain, the viewer notices both texture and distance more clearly.

## Guided Look
Ask students to study a sample artwork and discuss:

1. Which colors seem to move forward?
2. Which part has the strongest texture?
3. What makes one object look closer than another?

## Try It
Students create a quick sketch using:

- one warm color area
- one cool color area
- one textured section
- one overlapping object

## Exit Reflection
Students name one technique they used and explain how it changed their picture.
