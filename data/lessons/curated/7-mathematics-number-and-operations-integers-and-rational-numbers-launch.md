# Integers & Rational Numbers

## Learning Goal
- I can add, subtract, multiply, and divide integers and rational numbers.
- I can explain how signs change the result of an operation.
- I can solve real-world problems that involve positive and negative rational numbers.

## Introduction
By Grade 7, working with negative numbers is no longer only about locating them on a number line. You now use integers and rational numbers in full operations.

That means solving problems with:

- gains and losses
- deposits and withdrawals
- temperatures above and below zero
- fractions and decimals with signs

The sign of a number matters because it tells direction relative to `0`. The operation matters because it changes how those signed quantities combine.

In this lesson, focus on three questions:

1. How do I add and subtract rational numbers with signs?
2. How do sign rules work in multiplication and division?
3. How can I interpret the result in context?

## Key Vocabulary
**Integer**: A whole number, its opposite, or `0`.

**Rational number**: A number that can be written as a fraction of integers, including integers, fractions, and terminating or repeating decimals.

**Additive inverse**: A number and its opposite; together they add to `0`.

**Absolute value**: The distance from `0` on the number line.

**Quotient**: The result of division.

**Product**: The result of multiplication.

## Adding and Subtracting Signed Numbers
Addition and subtraction with rational numbers depend on direction and distance.

Key patterns:

- adding a positive moves right on the number line
- adding a negative moves left on the number line
- subtracting a number is the same as adding its opposite

That last idea is especially important:

`a - b = a + (-b)`

It turns subtraction into a form of addition, which makes the structure easier to track.

## Worked Example 1: Adding Integers
Find:

`-6 + 9`

Step 1: Compare signs.

The numbers have different signs, so subtract their absolute values:

`9 - 6 = 3`

Step 2: Keep the sign of the number with the greater absolute value.

`9` has the greater absolute value, and it is positive.

So:

`-6 + 9 = 3`

Interpretation:

If you lose `6` points and then gain `9` points, your total change is `+3`.

## Worked Example 2: Subtracting Rational Numbers
Find:

`4.5 - (-2.3)`

Step 1: Rewrite subtraction as addition.

`4.5 + 2.3`

Step 2: Add the decimals.

`6.8`

So:

`4.5 - (-2.3) = 6.8`

Subtracting a negative increases the value because you are adding the opposite.

## Worked Example 3: Multiplying Rational Numbers
Find:

`(-3/4) x 8`

Step 1: Multiply the absolute values.

`3/4 x 8 = 6`

Step 2: Apply the sign rule.

A negative times a positive is negative.

So:

`(-3/4) x 8 = -6`

This matches the idea of taking eight groups of negative three-fourths, or scaling a positive amount in the negative direction.

## Worked Example 4: Dividing Signed Numbers
Find:

`-18 / 6`

Step 1: Divide the absolute values.

`18 / 6 = 3`

Step 2: Apply the sign rule.

A negative divided by a positive is negative.

So:

`-18 / 6 = -3`

The same sign rules apply in multiplication and division:

- same signs give a positive result
- different signs give a negative result

## Multi-Step Example in Context
A scuba diver starts at `-12` meters. The diver rises `4.5` meters, then descends `2` meters.

Find the diver's final depth.

Step 1: Start at `-12`.

Step 2: Add the rise.

`-12 + 4.5 = -7.5`

Step 3: Add the descent as a negative change.

`-7.5 + (-2) = -9.5`

So the final depth is:

`-9.5` meters

Interpretation:

The diver is still below sea level, but not as deep as at the start.

## Worked Example 5: Combining Fractions With Signs
Find:

`-2/5 + 7/10`

Step 1: Rewrite with a common denominator.

`-2/5 = -4/10`

Step 2: Add:

`-4/10 + 7/10 = 3/10`

So:

`-2/5 + 7/10 = 3/10`

The common denominator helps you combine the signed parts accurately.

## Common Mistake: Forgetting That Subtracting a Negative Changes Direction
Students often see:

`5 - (-3)`

and answer:

`2`

That mistake happens because they ignore the sign of the second number.

The correct reasoning is:

`5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = 8`

Another common mistake is assuming every answer with a negative number must be negative. The operation and the signs together determine the result.

## Summary
Grade 7 work with integers and rational numbers includes full operations, not just ordering and graphing.

To solve well:

1. use subtraction as adding the opposite
2. compare absolute values when adding numbers with different signs
3. use sign rules carefully in multiplication and division
4. interpret the final value in context, not just as a bare number

When you keep track of both magnitude and direction, signed rational numbers become much more manageable.
