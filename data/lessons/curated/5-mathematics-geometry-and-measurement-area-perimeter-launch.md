# Area/Perimeter

## Learning Goal
- I can explain the difference between area and perimeter.
- I can calculate area and perimeter of rectangles accurately.
- I can compare shapes to see how perimeter and area can change in different ways.

## Introduction
By Grade 5, students should be able to calculate both area and perimeter and explain why they are not interchangeable.

Two shapes can have:

- the same perimeter but different areas
- the same area but different perimeters

That is why a visual model matters before students jump to a formula.

Students can build rectangles in the [Desmos Geometry tool](https://www.desmos.com/geometry) to test which side-length changes affect perimeter, area, or both.

## Key Vocabulary
**Perimeter**: The total distance around a figure.

**Area**: The number of square units that cover a figure.

**Dimension**: A measurement such as length or width.

## Worked Example
Rectangle `A` is `8` units by `2` units.

Perimeter:

`8 + 2 + 8 + 2 = 20`

Area:

`8 x 2 = 16`

Rectangle `B` is `5` units by `5` units.

Perimeter:

`5 + 5 + 5 + 5 = 20`

Area:

`5 x 5 = 25`

Both rectangles have perimeter `20`, but their areas are different.

## Summary
Strong measurement reasoning means:

1. naming which attribute the problem asks for
2. using the correct calculation
3. checking whether the answer matches the visual meaning of the shape
