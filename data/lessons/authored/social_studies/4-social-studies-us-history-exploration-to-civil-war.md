# Exploration to Civil War

## Learning Goal

- I can use chronology to place major events in order.
- I can identify one historical change shown in a map or source.
- I can support a simple cause-and-effect claim with evidence.

## Introduction

The United States changed dramatically between the era of exploration and the Civil War. Maps show territory changing over time. Timelines show important events in sequence. A short diary or document can reveal how people experienced those changes.

In this lesson, you will examine a map, a timeline, and a short historical excerpt. Your job is to explain one change over time and how that change helped increase conflict.

## Key Vocabulary

**chronology**: the order in which events happen
**territory**: land controlled by a country or government
**expansion**: the growth of land, power, or settlement
**conflict**: serious disagreement or struggle
**evidence**: a source detail that supports a claim

## Source 1: Map and Timeline Pair

The paired source might show:

- the original colonies
- later land growth
- major dates such as independence and westward expansion

This source helps students see both place and sequence.

## Source 2: Historical Excerpt

A short diary, speech, or public statement may describe:

- moving west
- debates over power
- growing disagreements about slavery

This source adds perspective. It shows how people at the time viewed change and conflict.

## Building a Cause-and-Effect Claim

Observation:

- The map shows land growth over time.
- The timeline places major events in order.
- The excerpt shows that some of these changes caused disagreement.

Reasonable claim:

- Expansion increased conflict because new land raised hard questions about power and slavery.

Students should distinguish between what the source directly shows and what the evidence supports as an explanation.

## Why Multiple Sources Help

One source alone cannot explain the whole period. The map shows physical change. The timeline shows sequence. The excerpt shows perspective. Together, the sources help students explain why the nation moved toward deeper conflict before the Civil War.

## Summary

The period from exploration to the Civil War includes major changes in territory, power, and conflict. A map and timeline provide context. A historical excerpt adds evidence from the time. Together, they support a stronger cause-and-effect explanation.

Before making a claim, ask:

- What changed over time?
- What source detail shows that change?
- How does the evidence support my explanation?
