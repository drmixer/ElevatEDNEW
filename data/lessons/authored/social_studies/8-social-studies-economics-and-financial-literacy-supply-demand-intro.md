# Supply and Demand (Intro)

## Learning Goal

- I can interpret what a market graph directly shows.
- I can explain how a scenario changes supply or demand.
- I can support an economic explanation with evidence.

## Introduction

Real markets change for different reasons. A poor harvest can reduce supply. A trend on social media can increase demand. If students do not separate the graph from the scenario, they may confuse what happened with why it happened.

In this lesson, you will use a market graph and a short event scenario to explain how supply and demand shape outcomes.

## Key Vocabulary

**supply**: how much sellers offer
**demand**: how much buyers want
**market outcome**: the result of how buyers and sellers interact
**scenario**: a short description of an event affecting the market
**evidence**: source details that support a claim

## Source 1: Market Graph

The graph may show:

- a shift in price
- a change in quantity

This source directly shows the market pattern. It does not identify the cause by itself.

## Source 2: Event Scenario

Example scenario:

- a drought reduces the amount of wheat farmers can grow

This source suggests a supply change because it describes producers being able to offer less.

## Connecting Pattern and Cause

Observation:

- The graph shows less quantity at a higher price.
- The scenario describes reduced production.

Reasonable conclusion:

- Supply likely decreased, helping explain the market outcome.

Students should distinguish this from a demand story. If the scenario instead described many more buyers wanting wheat, the source would suggest demand rather than supply.

## Summary

Market analysis is stronger when students pair a graph with a scenario. The graph shows the economic pattern. The scenario suggests the likely cause. Together, the sources help students explain how supply and demand affect real outcomes.

Before making a claim, ask:

- What does the graph directly show?
- What change does the scenario describe?
- Does the evidence point to supply, demand, or both?
