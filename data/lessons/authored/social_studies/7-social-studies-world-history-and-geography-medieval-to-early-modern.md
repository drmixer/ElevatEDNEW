# Medieval to Early Modern

## Learning Goal

- I can interpret a map or document in context.
- I can explain one major transformation from the era.
- I can support a claim about change with evidence.

## Introduction

The move from medieval to early modern history involved changes in trade, belief, political power, and contact across regions. Historians often use maps and documents together to show both structure and perspective.

In this lesson, you will analyze a map and a document excerpt. Your job is to explain one important transformation from the era using evidence from both sources.

## Key Vocabulary

**transformation**: a major change over time
**context**: the larger situation around a source
**state power**: the authority exercised by rulers or governments
**reform**: an effort to improve or change a system
**evidence**: the details that support a claim

## Source 1: Map

A map may show trade routes, political boundaries, or centers of power. It directly shows how places were connected and where change was concentrated.

## Source 2: Document Excerpt

A document may describe trade, reform, belief, or political authority. It adds voice and perspective to the larger geographic pattern.

## Using Both Sources

Observation:

- The map shows expanding or changing connections.
- The document reveals what one person or institution saw as important.

Reasonable claim:

- The era was transformed by shifting connections, ideas, or forms of power.

For example, if a map shows stronger trade links and the document describes a ruler or reformer responding to those links, students can explain one transformation of the era with evidence.

The strongest answers do not treat the map and document separately. They use each source to strengthen the other.

## Summary

Medieval to early modern history is easier to explain when students combine geographic and written evidence. Maps reveal patterns of connection. Documents reveal perspective and detail. Together, they support stronger claims about transformation.

Before making a claim, ask:

- What pattern does the map show?
- What does the document add?
- What transformation do the sources support?
