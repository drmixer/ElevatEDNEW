# World Regions (Physical/Human)

## Learning Goal

- I can distinguish physical features from human features.
- I can use map symbols and labels to answer questions.
- I can describe one way people adapt to a region.

## Introduction

Look at a map with mountains, rivers, roads, and cities. Some parts of the map were made by nature. Other parts were built by people. To understand a region, students need to notice both.

In this lesson, you will read maps that show physical and human features. Your job is to explain which features belong to nature, which belong to people, and how they connect.

## Key Vocabulary

**physical feature**: a natural part of Earth, such as a river or mountain
**human feature**: something people build or create, such as a road or city
**region**: an area with shared features
**legend**: the part of a map that explains symbols
**adapt**: to change behavior or choices to fit a place

## Source 1: World Map

A world map might show:

- mountain ranges
- rivers
- deserts
- major cities

This source helps students identify both natural landforms and places where people live.

## Source 2: Regional Feature Map

A regional map might include:

- roads
- farms
- towns near water

This source helps students see how human features often connect to physical ones. Roads may follow valleys. Towns may grow near rivers or coasts.

## Comparing Physical and Human Features

Observation:

- Mountains and rivers are physical features.
- Roads and cities are human features.

Reasonable explanation:

- People often build where physical features make travel, farming, or settlement easier.

Students should use the legend and labels instead of relying on guesses.

## Why Geography Matters

Regions are shaped by both land and people. Physical features can affect where people settle, how they move, and what jobs they do. Human features show how people respond to those conditions.

## Summary

Physical features are part of nature. Human features are created by people. Maps help students compare both kinds of features and explain how they connect in a region.

Before answering, ask:

- Which feature is natural?
- Which feature was built by people?
- What evidence on the map shows how they connect?
