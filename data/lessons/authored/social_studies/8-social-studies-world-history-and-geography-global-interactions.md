# Global Interactions

## Learning Goal

- I can interpret a source showing regional interaction.
- I can explain one consequence of global exchange.
- I can support a claim with evidence from multiple sources.

## Introduction

Global interaction is about networks. Trade, migration, belief, and information do not stay in one region once routes connect societies. But those same networks can spread unequal power, conflict, or disease along with ideas and goods.

In this lesson, you will analyze a world trade or migration map and a supporting source. Your task is to explain how networks of exchange shape societies.

## Key Vocabulary

**network**: a system of connected places or people
**exchange**: the movement of goods, ideas, beliefs, or practices across regions
**interaction**: contact that allows people or places to influence one another
**consequence**: an effect or result
**evidence**: source details that support a claim

## Source 1: Trade or Migration Map

The map may show:

- major routes
- important hubs
- direction of movement

This source directly shows the structure of the network.

## Source 2: Supporting Source

A case study, travel account, or policy source can show what moved through that network and what effects followed.

## Interpreting the Sources Together

Observation:

- The map shows repeated connections among regions.
- The supporting source shows a concrete effect of those connections.

Reasonable claim:

- Networks of exchange helped reshape societies by spreading something important across regions.

The strongest claims recognize that consequences may be mixed. The same network can spread opportunity and hardship at the same time.

## Summary

Global interactions are best understood through more than one source. A map reveals the pattern of connection. A supporting source reveals what those connections meant in human terms. Together, they help students explain how exchange shapes societies.

When analyzing interaction, ask:

- What pattern does the map show?
- What moved through the network?
- What consequence did the source describe?
