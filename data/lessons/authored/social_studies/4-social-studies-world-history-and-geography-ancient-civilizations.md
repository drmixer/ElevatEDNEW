# Ancient Civilizations

## Learning Goal

- I can locate an ancient civilization on a map.
- I can describe what an artifact reveals about daily life.
- I can use source evidence to make a supported inference.

## Introduction

Ancient civilizations developed in specific environments, and historians learn about them through evidence. A river valley map can show where people lived. Artifact images can show what people built, traded, or valued.

In this lesson, you will analyze a map and two artifact images. Your job is to explain how location and artifacts help historians understand an ancient civilization.

## Key Vocabulary

**civilization**: a large organized society
**artifact**: an object made or used by people in the past
**river valley**: low land around a river, often useful for farming and settlement
**inference**: a conclusion based on evidence
**context**: the larger place and situation around a source

## Source 1: River-Valley Map

A map might show:

- a major river
- nearby farmland
- cities or settlements along the water

This source helps students understand why people may have chosen that place to live and build a society.

## Source 2: Artifact Images

Artifact images might include:

- a clay tablet
- a tool
- a decorated pot

These sources show evidence of writing, work, trade, belief, or daily routines.

## Using Map Context and Artifact Evidence

Observation:

- Settlements appear near water.
- The artifacts show that people made useful and organized objects.

Reasonable inference:

- Access to water supported farming, travel, or trade.
- The artifacts suggest that the civilization had systems for work, storage, or communication.

Students should explain how the map and artifacts support each other.

## Why Historians Compare Sources

One artifact can show a detail. A map can show location. When students use both, they can make a stronger explanation about how environment and human choices shaped civilization life.

## Summary

Ancient civilizations can be studied through both geography and artifacts. A map provides place context. Artifact images provide evidence of daily life and organization. Together, these sources help students make supported historical inferences.

Before answering, ask:

- What does the map show about location?
- What do the artifacts show about society?
- What inference fits both sources?
