# Producers/Consumers

## Learning Goal

- I can identify producers and consumers in a chain of exchange.
- I can explain how a good moves from creation to purchase.
- I can use source details to support an economic explanation.

## Introduction

When students buy a T-shirt, they usually see only the final product. They do not always think about the workers, shipping steps, and store choices that helped move the good from producer to consumer.

In this lesson, you will analyze a supply-chain card set and a receipt. Your job is to trace how a product moves through a market and explain which people are acting as producers and which are acting as consumers.

## Key Vocabulary

**producer**: a person or business that makes, transports, or sells a good or service
**consumer**: a person who buys or uses a good or service
**supply chain**: the sequence of steps that moves a product from production to purchase
**exchange**: the movement of goods, services, or money between people
**evidence**: a source detail that supports an explanation

## Source 1: Supply-Chain Cards

A card set might show:

- cotton being grown
- cloth being made
- shirts being packed and shipped
- shirts being sold in a store

This source shows that production is often a sequence, not a single step.

## Source 2: Receipt

A receipt might show:

- the item purchased
- the store name
- the price paid

This source helps students identify the consumer end of the chain.

## Tracing the Path

Observation:

- Several people help create, move, and sell the shirt before it is bought.
- The receipt shows the final purchase.

Reasonable explanation:

- Producers include the people and businesses that make, ship, or sell the product.
- The consumer is the person who buys the product at the end of the exchange.

Students should explain how the roles connect instead of treating them as separate vocabulary words.

## Why the Chain Matters

Understanding the full chain helps students see that markets depend on many linked decisions. Producers try to move goods efficiently. Consumers make the final purchasing choice. A source-based explanation shows how those roles fit together in one exchange.

## Summary

Supply-chain cards help students see the path from production to sale. A receipt shows the consumer purchase at the end of that path. Together, these sources support a clearer explanation of producers and consumers.

Before answering, ask:

- What step in the chain am I describing?
- Who is acting as a producer there?
- What source detail proves who the consumer is?
