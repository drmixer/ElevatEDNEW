# Supply and Demand (Intro)

## Learning Goal

- I can interpret what a market graph directly shows.
- I can explain whether a scenario changes supply or demand.
- I can support an economic explanation with evidence.

## Introduction

Snow shovels often become more expensive right before a major storm. Does that happen because stores suddenly have fewer shovels, because more people want them, or both?

In this lesson, you will use a graph and a short market scenario to explain how changes in supply or demand affect price and quantity.

## Key Vocabulary

**supply**: how much sellers offer
**demand**: how much buyers want to buy
**market graph**: a visual source showing how price and quantity relate
**scenario**: a short description of an economic event or condition
**evidence**: the details in a source that support a claim

## Source 1: Market Graph

A graph may show that:

- price rises from one point to another
- quantity changes as the price changes

The graph directly shows the pattern, but not the whole story behind it. Students need the scenario to interpret the shift more carefully.

## Source 2: Market Scenario

Example scenario:

- A storm warning causes many families to buy shovels at the same time.

This source suggests an increase in demand. The scenario does not describe sellers making more or fewer shovels. Instead, it describes a change in what buyers want.

## Observation Before Interpretation

Observation:

- The graph shows a higher price.
- The scenario describes more buyers entering the market.

Reasonable conclusion:

- Demand likely increased.

Students should avoid mixing up the graph pattern with the cause. The graph shows what happened in the market. The scenario helps explain why.

## Summary

Economic reasoning works best when students use both a graph and a scenario. The graph shows price and quantity changes. The scenario suggests what may have caused the shift. Together, the sources support a clearer explanation of supply or demand.

Before making a claim, ask:

- What does the graph directly show?
- What does the scenario describe?
- Which market force best fits the evidence?
