# Producers/Consumers

## Learning Goal

- I can identify producer and consumer roles in a market exchange.
- I can explain how producers try to attract consumers.
- I can support an economic claim with source evidence.

## Introduction

Advertisements are not neutral. They are made for a purpose: to persuade consumers to buy something. That means students can learn about producer goals by studying how products are presented, not just what is sold.

In this lesson, you will examine an advertisement, a product tag, and a receipt. Your job is to explain how producers try to influence consumers and how the sources reveal that relationship.

## Key Vocabulary

**producer**: a person or business that makes, markets, or sells a good or service
**consumer**: a person who buys or uses a good or service
**advertisement**: a message designed to persuade people to buy something
**audience**: the group a message is aimed at
**evidence**: a source detail that supports a claim

## Source 1: Advertisement and Product Tag

These sources might show:

- bright language about taste or value
- a product name and brand
- information meant to attract attention

This evidence helps students identify the producer's goal and intended audience.

## Source 2: Receipt

A receipt might show:

- the item purchased
- the price
- the final transaction

This source helps students identify the consumer side of the exchange.

## Explaining the Relationship

Observation:

- The advertisement is written to persuade.
- The receipt proves that a purchase happened.

Reasonable claim:

- Producers use advertising and product presentation to influence consumer choices.

Supporting explanation:

- The receipt shows the consumer response at the end of the process.

Students should cite specific words, images, or details from the advertisement instead of giving a vague answer such as "it looked good."

## Why Source Purpose Matters

An advertisement is useful because it has a clear purpose. It is trying to shape behavior. A receipt is useful because it records the result of a purchase. Together, these sources help students explain that producers and consumers affect each other in a market.

## Summary

Advertisements reveal how producers try to attract buyers. Product tags and receipts show how those market choices lead to purchases. Together, these sources help students explain the link between producer decisions and consumer actions.

Before answering, ask:

- What is the producer trying to make consumers do?
- What evidence shows that goal?
- How does the receipt show the consumer role?
