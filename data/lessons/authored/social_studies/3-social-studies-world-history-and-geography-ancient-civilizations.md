# Ancient Civilizations

## Learning Goal

- I can identify what an artifact can tell us about a civilization.
- I can use a map to locate an ancient civilization.
- I can make a simple inference from source evidence.

## Introduction

Historians do not learn about ancient civilizations by guessing. They study maps, artifacts, monuments, and other evidence left behind by people long ago. Even one object can reveal something important about daily life.

In this lesson, you will use an artifact card and a map to learn how historians study ancient civilizations. Your job is to describe what the source shows directly and what it suggests about the people who lived there.

## Key Vocabulary

**artifact**: an object made or used by people in the past
**civilization**: a large organized society
**infer**: to make a careful idea from evidence
**map**: a source that shows where places are located
**monument**: a large structure built to remember or honor something

## Source 1: Artifact Card

An artifact card might show:

- a clay pot
- an ancient tool
- a piece of writing

This source helps students ask what the object was used for. Was it for storing food, farming, building, or communication?

## Source 2: Map of an Ancient Civilization

A map might show:

- a river
- nearby landforms
- the location of cities or settlements

This source gives context. It helps students understand where people lived and why that place may have supported farming, travel, or trade.

## Using Evidence Carefully

Observation:

- The artifact has a certain shape or material.
- The map shows where the civilization lived.

Reasonable inference:

- If people lived near a river, water may have helped with farming or travel.
- If an artifact is carefully decorated, it may have been important in daily life or belief.

For example, a grain pot found near a river settlement suggests people depended on farming and food storage.

Students should separate what the source shows directly from what the source only suggests.

## Why Historians Use More Than One Source

An artifact by itself can tell only part of the story. A map by itself can tell only part of the story. Together, the sources help students build a stronger explanation about how people lived, worked, and organized their society.

## Summary

Artifacts and maps are important tools for studying ancient civilizations. An artifact shows evidence of human activity. A map shows where a civilization lived. Together, they help students make careful inferences about life in the past.

Before answering, ask:

- What does the artifact directly show?
- What does the map add?
- Is my idea an observation or an inference?
