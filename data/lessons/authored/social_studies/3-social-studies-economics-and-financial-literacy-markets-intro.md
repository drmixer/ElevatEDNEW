# Markets (Intro)

## Learning Goal

- I can explain what a market is.
- I can identify buyers and sellers in a simple source.
- I can use a price list to answer basic questions.

## Introduction

At a farmers market, one person sells apples, another sells bread, and another sells flowers. Families walk by, compare prices, and decide what to buy. A market is not just a place with tables. It is a place where buyers and sellers exchange goods or services.

In this lesson, you will examine a market price board and a market scene. Your job is to notice who is selling, who is buying, and what the source shows about how a market works.

## Key Vocabulary

**market**: a place or system where buyers and sellers exchange goods or services
**buyer**: a person who purchases something
**seller**: a person who offers something for sale
**good**: an object people can buy or sell
**price**: the amount of money something costs

## Source 1: Farmers Market Price Board

A price board might list:

- apples: `$2` per bag
- bread: `$4` per loaf
- flowers: `$5` per bunch

This source directly shows what is being sold and how much each item costs. It helps students answer basic questions such as which item costs more or what a family could buy with a certain amount of money.

## Source 2: Market Scene

A market picture might show:

- a seller standing behind a table
- signs with prices
- shoppers carrying bags

The picture adds context. It shows that markets involve people making choices. Sellers provide goods. Buyers decide what they want and what they can afford.

## What the Sources Show

Observation:

- Different goods have different prices.
- Sellers offer goods for sale.
- Buyers compare and choose items.

Reasonable explanation:

- A market helps people exchange goods.
- Prices help buyers and sellers understand what something costs.

Not supported:

- "Everyone at the market wants the same thing."

The source shows exchange, but not every thought in each buyer's mind.

## Why Markets Matter

Markets help communities because they connect people who make or provide goods with people who want to buy them. Even a simple price board gives useful economic information. It shows that choices in a market depend on what is offered and what it costs.

## Summary

A market is where buyers and sellers meet to exchange goods or services. A price board shows what is being sold and for how much. A market scene shows the roles buyers and sellers play. Together, these sources help students explain how a market works.

Before answering, ask:

- What goods are being sold?
- Who is buying and who is selling?
- What detail from the source proves my answer?
