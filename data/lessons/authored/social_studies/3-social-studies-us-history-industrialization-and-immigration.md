# Industrialization & Immigration

## Learning Goal

- I can describe one change caused by industrialization.
- I can describe one challenge or opportunity connected to immigration.
- I can use details from a photo or document to support an answer.

## Introduction

When factories grew and more immigrants arrived in the United States, cities changed quickly. Streets became busier, jobs changed, and many families had to adjust to new opportunities and new challenges.

In this lesson, you will study a historical photo and an arrival record. Your job is to notice what each source directly shows, then explain how work and city life were changing.

## Key Vocabulary

**industrialization**: the growth of machine-based factory production
**immigration**: moving to a new country to live
**factory**: a place where goods are made, often with machines
**opportunity**: a good chance for work or a better life
**evidence**: a source detail that supports an answer

## Source 1: Historical Photo

A historical photo might show:

- many workers in one building
- large machines
- crowded city streets

This source directly shows that work and city spaces were becoming more crowded and more organized around factories.

## Source 2: Arrival Record

A simple arrival card or checklist might show:

- the name of a person entering the country
- where that person came from
- a reason for travel or arrival

This source helps students see that immigration involved real people moving for work, safety, or opportunity.

## Observation Before Inference

Observation:

- The photo shows machines, workers, and crowding.
- The arrival record shows movement into a new country.

Reasonable explanation:

- Industrialization changed how and where many people worked.
- Immigration added new people and experiences to American cities.

Not supported:

- "Every immigrant had the same experience."

One source can show a pattern, but it cannot tell every story.

## Why These Changes Matter

Industrialization and immigration were connected to major changes in daily life. Factories could create more goods, but they also changed working conditions. Growing cities could offer jobs and opportunity, but they could also become crowded and difficult places to live.

## Summary

A historical photo shows visible change in work and city life. An arrival record shows people coming to the United States for new reasons and new possibilities. Together, the sources help students explain how industrialization and immigration reshaped American life.

Before answering, ask:

- What detail do I see directly in the source?
- What change does that detail suggest?
- What claim would go beyond the evidence?
