# Exploration to Civil War

## Learning Goal

- I can place key events in chronological order.
- I can use a map or timeline to answer a historical question.
- I can explain one cause-and-effect relationship from the period.

## Introduction

History is easier to understand when students can see both time and place. A timeline shows what happened first, next, and later. A map shows where people explored, settled, and fought.

In this lesson, you will examine a simple timeline and a map to build a picture of major events from exploration through the Civil War. Your job is to use those sources to explain sequence and change.

## Key Vocabulary

**exploration**: traveling to learn about or claim new places
**colony**: a settlement controlled by another country
**independence**: freedom from another country's control
**Civil War**: a war between groups in the same country
**timeline**: a source that shows events in time order

## Source 1: Timeline

A basic timeline might include:

- European exploration
- colonies growing in North America
- the American Revolution
- the Civil War

This source helps students see that the Civil War came after earlier periods of settlement, independence, and growth.

## Source 2: Map of Change Over Time

A map of the period might show:

- the original colonies
- land beyond the colonies
- how the United States grew larger over time

This source gives place clues. It shows that the nation changed across time, not just on paper but on the land itself.

## Using Sequence and Place

Observation:

- Exploration happened before independence.
- The country expanded after the colonies formed.

Reasonable cause-and-effect idea:

- As the country grew and changed, new disagreements also grew.

Students should not try to explain every conflict from one map. The goal is to use the timeline and map together to support a simple historical idea.

## Why Time Order Matters

When students know what came first, they can explain what later events grew out of earlier ones. A timeline prevents random guessing. A map adds evidence about where those changes were happening.

## Summary

The period from exploration to the Civil War includes major changes in both time and place. A timeline helps students put events in order. A map helps students see where those events happened and how the country changed. Together, the sources support stronger historical explanations.

Before answering, ask:

- Which event happened first?
- What changed over time on the map?
- What source detail supports my answer?
