# Industrialization & Immigration

## Learning Goal

- I can describe one feature of industrial work.
- I can describe one reason people immigrated.
- I can use evidence from a visual or document to support a historical claim.

## Introduction

Industrialization and immigration changed American cities quickly. New factories created jobs, but they also changed working conditions. Many immigrants arrived hoping for opportunity, but city life could also be crowded and difficult.

In this lesson, you will examine a factory photo and an immigration document. Your job is to identify direct source details, then explain what those details suggest about change in work and city life.

## Key Vocabulary

**industrialization**: the growth of machine-based production in factories
**immigration**: moving to a new country to live
**document**: a written or recorded source from a time period
**observation**: something the source directly shows
**inference**: a careful conclusion based on evidence

## Source 1: Factory Photo

A factory image might show:

- rows of machines
- many workers close together
- a busy indoor workspace

This source directly shows what industrial work could look like.

## Source 2: Immigration Checkpoint Document

A checkpoint record might show:

- names of new arrivals
- countries of origin
- reasons for travel or entry

This source helps students connect migration to the growth of American cities and workplaces.

## Observation and Inference

Observation:

- The photo shows machine-based work in a crowded setting.
- The document shows people entering the country from many places.

Reasonable inference:

- Industrial growth created demand for workers.
- Immigration brought new people into those changing cities.

For example, a factory photo with crowded machines and a checkpoint record showing new arrivals together suggest that growing industries needed more workers.

Students should not assume every person had the same experience. The sources show patterns, not every individual story.

## Why Perspective Matters

A photo reveals what can be seen. A document reveals official recorded information. Together, those sources provide stronger evidence than either one alone. Students can explain how work and migration reshaped city life by combining visual and written evidence.

## Summary

Industrialization changed how people worked. Immigration changed who was arriving and helping build city life. A factory photo and an immigration document help students explain both changes with source evidence.

Before answering, ask:

- What do I see directly in the photo?
- What information does the document add?
- What historical claim does the evidence support?
