# Reconstruction to WWII

## Learning Goal

- I can identify a major development from Reconstruction to World War II.
- I can use source evidence to explain change over time.
- I can support a claim with a document detail and timeline context.

## Introduction

The period from Reconstruction to World War II includes many major changes: rebuilding after the Civil War, industrial growth, reform movements, and increasing involvement in world affairs. Historians do not study these events as random pieces. They look for how one development helped shape the next.

In this lesson, you will read a timeline and a short historical document. Your job is to explain one turning point from the era using evidence from both sources.

## Key Vocabulary

**Reconstruction**: the period after the Civil War when the nation faced major questions about rebuilding and rights
**turning point**: an event or development that changes what happens next
**context**: the larger time, place, and situation around a source
**document**: a written source from or about a historical period
**change over time**: the way conditions, ideas, or systems develop across years

## Source 1: Timeline of the Era

A timeline might include:

- Reconstruction policies after the Civil War
- industrial expansion in the late 1800s
- reform movements in the Progressive Era
- U.S. involvement in World War I
- the Great Depression
- entry into World War II

This source helps students see sequence. It shows that the era includes connected changes rather than isolated events.

## Source 2: Historical Document

A short document from the era might be:

- a reform speech
- a labor demand
- a public argument about national policy

The document can show point of view, concern, or conflict from one moment in the period. The timeline helps students place that document in a broader pattern.

## Interpreting the Evidence

Observation:

- The document argues for change in one part of society.
- The timeline shows the nation was experiencing broader political and economic shifts.

Reasonable claim:

- The document reflects a larger turning point in how Americans responded to industrial or political change.

Students should avoid making claims that are too broad for the evidence. One source may show a problem or argument, but the timeline is what helps explain how that issue fits the larger era.

## Why This Era Matters

This period matters because it helps explain how the United States became more industrial, more urban, and more involved in national and global problems. It also shows that growth often produced conflict over rights, work, and reform.

## Summary

To study Reconstruction through World War II well, students need both context and evidence. A timeline shows the sequence of change. A document shows how people experienced or argued about those changes. Together, they support stronger historical claims.

Before making a claim, ask:

- What turning point am I focusing on?
- What detail from the document supports it?
- Where does the timeline place that change?
