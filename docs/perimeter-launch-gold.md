# Perimeter (Intro) Launch Lesson

## Learning Goal
- I can explain what perimeter means.
- I can add side lengths to find the perimeter of a shape.
- I can use perimeter in simple real-world examples.

## Introduction
Imagine you are putting a border around a garden or a ribbon around a picture frame. To know how much you need, you have to measure all the way around the outside edge. That outside distance is called the **perimeter**.

Today, we will look at squares, rectangles, and triangles. We will practice adding the side lengths so you can find the perimeter with confidence.

## Key Vocabulary
**Perimeter**: The total distance around the outside of a shape.

**Side**: One straight edge of a shape.

**Corner**: The point where two sides meet.

**Rectangle**: A shape with 2 long sides and 2 short sides.

**Square**: A shape with 4 equal sides.

## What Is Perimeter?
Perimeter is not the space inside a shape. Perimeter is the distance **around** the shape.

If you traced the outside edge of a shape with your finger, the path your finger makes would show the perimeter.

When you find perimeter, follow these steps:

1. Look at each side length.
2. Make sure you count every side one time.
3. Add the side lengths together.
4. Write the answer with the unit, such as feet or inches.

## Square Example
Suppose a square sandbox has sides that are each 3 feet long.

- Side 1 = 3 feet
- Side 2 = 3 feet
- Side 3 = 3 feet
- Side 4 = 3 feet

Add all the sides:

- 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 12

So, the perimeter is **12 feet**.

## Rectangle Example
Suppose a rectangle is 4 feet long and 2 feet wide.

- Long side = 4 feet
- Short side = 2 feet
- Long side = 4 feet
- Short side = 2 feet

Add all the sides:

- 4 + 2 + 4 + 2 = 12

So, the perimeter is **12 feet**.

## Triangle Example
Suppose a triangle has side lengths of 2 feet, 3 feet, and 4 feet.

Add all the sides:

- 2 + 3 + 4 = 9

So, the perimeter is **9 feet**.

### Real-World Use
People use perimeter in real life when they need to measure the outside edge of something.

For example:
- a gardener can measure the perimeter of a flower bed to know how much fencing to buy
- a person can measure the perimeter of a picture frame to know how much ribbon or border is needed

In the practice part of this lesson, you will solve your own perimeter questions and add the side lengths yourself.

## Summary
Today, you learned that perimeter is the total distance around the outside of a shape. You practiced finding the perimeter of squares, rectangles, and triangles by adding all the side lengths together.

When you see a shape, remember this:

- perimeter means around the outside
- count every side one time
- add carefully

That is how you find the perimeter.
